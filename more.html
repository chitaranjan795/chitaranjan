Let's Know The Freedom Fighters Of Odisha

Jayee Rajguru :~

Jayakrushna Rajguru Mohapatra popularly known as Rajguru was a prominent figure of the Indian Independence movement in the state of Odisha. He borned on 29 october 1739.A princely-priest by profession at the court of the raja of Khurdha, Rajguru was the first person to revolt against the British Raj in the province. While colabarating with the marathas for recaptured the British occupied province, a maratha messenger was caught by the British army and Rajguru's secret strategies got exposed.Upon failure of his removal from the king's court, the British force attacked on the fort of khurdha and captured Rajguru. Later, his legs tying on two banyan tree and split them and his body is divided into two part.

Veer Surendra Sai :~

Veer Surendra sai was born on 23 January 1809 at Sambalpur. He was an Indian freedom fighter who sacrificed his life fighting against the British. Every year in 23 January odisha people as well as indian people celebrate his birthday. On 2009 'Paschim Odisha Agrani Sangathan' of Bhubaneswar brought out a book titled Surendra Sai edited by Dr. Chitrasen Pasayat and Dr. Prabhas Kumar Singh. Also, another book titled Veer Surendra Sai: The Great Revolutionary edited by Dr. Chitrasen Pasayat and Sri Sasanka Sekhar Panda has been published by Anusheelan, Sambalpur. After spend 40 year of his life at the jail of Asirgarah he died on 28 february 1984

Baji Raut :~

Baji Rout is the youngest martyr of India. This 12 yr old boat boy was shot dead by British police when he courageously denied to ferry them across the Brahmani River on the night of 11 October 1938 at Nilakanthapur Ghat, Bhuban, Dhenkanal district. Baji Rout was the youngest son of a boatman on the Brahmani river. As an active member of the Banar Sena of Prajamandal (Party of People). He had volunteered to keep watch by the river at night. In memory of the yongest martyr of India, Utkala cultural Association at IIT Bombay honors one odia youth with Baji Raut Sanman

Laxman Naik :~

Laxman Naik was born on 22 November 1899. He was a civil rights activist of South eastern India. He worked as a subsidiary of the British Government. The tribals under their administration were treted badly by revenue officials, forest guiders and police constables, and were subjected to torture. Nayak organised the rebels successfully against exploitation by the officials of Jeypore Samasthanam. Responding to the call of Mahatma Gandhi, Nayak led a procession on 21 August 1942 and demonstrated peacefully in front of Mathili Police Station. The police however fired at the demonstrators indiscriminately, which killed forty and injured more than two hundred people. The administration implicated Nayak in a case of murder and the death sentence was pronounced on him on 13 November 1942. He was hanged on 29 March 1943 in Berhampur Jail

Subhash Chandra Bose :~

Subhash Chandra Bose was born on 23 January 1897 in Cuttack. He was the president of Indian National Congress (INS) on 1938-1939. He attempted to get rid of British rule in India during World War with the help of Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan. He fonded the Azad Hind Army or Indian National Army with the help of Japan. His famous was:-
             Give me blood,
             and I will give you freedom
At the age of 48 he died in Talwan on 18 August 1945, But many Indians believed that he didn't die that time.

Parbati Giri :~

Parbati Giri was born on 19 january 1926 at bargarah district. She introduced as the nickname Mother Teresa Of Western Odisha. She is a prominent freedom fighter of odisha as well as for India. Parbati Giri was just 16 when she was in the forefront of agitation following Mahatma Gandhi's "Quit India" call. Due to her anti-British government activities, she was imprisoned for two years. She continued to serve the nation socially after independence. She opened an orphanage at Palkmal village and devoted rest of her life for the welfare of orphans.

Sarala Devi :~

Sarala Devi was born on 9 August 1904. She was the first odia woman who join the Non-cooperation movement in 1921. She was an Indian Independence activist,feminist, social activist, and writer. She was the first odia woman who join the Non-cooperation movement in 1921. In Independent India, she became the first woman to be elected to the Odisha Legislative Assembly, as well as first woman Director of Cuttack co-operative Bank. She is the first female senate member of Utkal University, and the first odisha woman delegate of the Indian National Congress, and the Secretary of Utkal Sahitya Samaj at Cuttack from 1943-194.

Gopabandhu Das :~

Gopabandhu Das was born on 9 October 1877 at Suanda at Puri district. Everyone knew him as Utkalmani Gopabandhu Das. Subhash Chandra Bose called him the father of the national movement in Odisha. He became the first president of the Congress party in Odisha and brought Gandhiji to Odisha on 1921 cause of the Non-Cooperation movement. He was arrested by police on 1921 and again imprisoned for two years on 1922. He was released from Hazaribagh jail on 26 June 1924. Gopabandhu fell ill while attending a fund raising ceremony in Lahore, after this he never recoverd and died on 17 June 1928.

Madhusudan Das :~

Madhusudan Das was born on 28 April 1848 in Satybhamapur at Cuttack. Mostly people knew him as Utkal Gourab Madhusudan Das. He is a poet cum freedom fighter. He is the first Graduate and Advocate in Odisha. He founded Utkal Samilani which bought a revoulation in the social and industrial development of Odisha. He elected as a member of Legislative council of Bihar and Odisha province .He was appointed as Minister for local self Government, medical public health, public works in 1921. He was the first odia who became the both Legislative Council and the Central Legislative Assembly of India.

Harekrushna Mahatab :~

Harekrushna Mahatab was born on 21 November 1899. Everyone knew him as Utkal Keshari. He was the leader of the Indian National Congress, a notable figure in the Indian Independence of Movement and the first Chief Minister of Odisha from 1946-1950 and again 1956-1961.

Biju Pattnaik's real name is Bijayananda Pattnaik. Everyone knew him as Bhoomi putra of Odisha/Indonesia. He was keenly interested in to be a pilot. He was trained pilot, during the world war he joined the Royal Indian Air Force eventually and become the head of air transport command.
